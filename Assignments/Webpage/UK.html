1914 - Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand


In June 1914, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria was assassinated in Sarajevo, a Serbian nationalist an event that would trigger the outbreak of World War I.

Chain Reaction That Led to War:

  1. Austrian-Hungary blamed Serbia and declared war.
  2. Russia supported Serbia and mobilized its army.
  3. Germany supported Austria-Hungary and declared war on Russia.
  4. Germany declared war on France.
  5. Germany invaded Belgium to attack France.
  6. Britain entered the war to defend Belgium.


World War I Begins (1914)

Britain Enters the Great War


The United Kingdom entered World War I after Germany invaded Belgium. Britain mobilized its navy and army to defend Europe.

Achievement: Naval dominance through Royal Navy blockade.



Battle of the Somme (1916)

Costly Offensive


British forces launched a massive assault against German trenches.

Casualties: ~420,000 British.
Impact: First major use of tanks.



End of World War I (1918)

Victory at Great Cost


Britain emerged victorious but deeply scarred.

Casualties: ~900,000 dead.



End of World War I

With the help of Allies they marked the end of the first World War



Battle of Britain (1940)

Defending the Skies


RAF successfully defended Britain from German air assault.

Achievement: Prevented invasion.



D-Day (1944)

Return to Europe


British troops landed at Gold and Sword beaches.

Achievement: Opened Western Front.



Victory in Europe (1945)

War Ends in Europe


Germany surrendered unconditionally.

WWII Casualties: ~450,000.

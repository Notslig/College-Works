1914 - Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand


In June 1914, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria was assassinated in Sarajevo, a Serbian nationalist an event that would trigger the outbreak of World War I.

Chain Reaction That Led to War:

  1. Austrian-Hungary blamed Serbia and declared war.
  2. Russia supported Serbia and mobilized its army.
  3. Germany supported Austria-Hungary and declared war on Russia.
  4. Germany declared war on France.
  5. Germany invaded Belgium to attack France.
  6. Britain entered the war to defend Belgium.


Outbreak of World War I (1914)

Germany Enters a Two-Front War


Germany entered World War I following rising tensions in Europe. The German military prepared to fight France in the west and Russia in the east.

Casualties (WWI total): ~2 million German soldiers killed.
Achievement: Early advances using highly disciplined forces.



Battle of Verdun (1916)

A War of Attrition


Germany launched a massive offensive against French defenses at Verdun. The battle became one of the longest and bloodiest in history.

Casualties: ~700,000 combined.
Outcome: Failed to break French resistance.



German Surrender (1918)

Defeat and Revolution


Exhausted by war, blockades, and internal unrest, Germany surrendered. The Kaiser abdicated, and the German Empire collapsed.

Outcome: Treaty of Versailles imposed severe penalties.



This is the end of World War I

The signing of the Treaty of Versailles in 1919 officially ended World War I.



Invasion of Poland (1939)

World War II Begins


Germany invaded Poland using Blitzkrieg tactics—fast-moving tanks, aircraft, and infantry coordination.

Captured: Warsaw and major Polish cities.
Achievement: Demonstrated modern mechanized warfare.



Fall of France (1940)

Lightning Victory in the West


German forces bypassed the Maginot Line and rapidly defeated France. Paris fell within weeks.

Casualties: ~90,000 French killed.
Achievement: Control over Western Europe.



Battle of Britain (1940)

Failure to Gain Air Superiority


Germany attempted to destroy the Royal Air Force through sustained air attacks. British resistance prevented invasion.

Outcome: First major German defeat of WWII.



Operation Barbarossa (1941)

Invasion of the Soviet Union


Germany launched the largest invasion in history against the USSR. Initial advances captured vast territory.

Captured: Ukraine, Baltic regions.
Failure: Winter and Soviet resistance halted progress.



Battle of Stalingrad (1942–1943)

The Turning Point


German Sixth Army was encircled by Soviet forces. After months of brutal fighting, the army surrendered.

Casualties: ~300,000 Axis losses.
Impact: End of German offensive capability in the east.



Battle of Kursk (1943)

Final German Offensive in the East


Germany launched a massive armored assault against Soviet defenses. The attack failed due to deep defensive lines.

Outcome: Permanent loss of strategic initiative.



D-Day – Normandy Invasion (1944)

The Western Front Reopens


Allied forces landed in Normandy, opening a second major front. German defenses were overwhelmed.

Impact: Accelerated German defeat.



Battle of the Bulge (1944–1945)

Germany’s Last Gamble


Germany launched a surprise winter offensive in Belgium. Initial success was followed by Allied counterattacks.

Outcome: Heavy German losses with no strategic gain.



Battle of Berlin (1945)

The Final Collapse


Soviet forces stormed Berlin after encircling the city. Adolf Hitler committed suicide as resistance collapsed.

Outcome: Total military defeat.



German Surrender (May 7, 1945)

End of the Third Reich


Germany surrendered unconditionally to the Allied Powers. The war in Europe officially ended.

Casualties (WWII): ~6–7 million total.



End of Hitler and the Third Reich

Adolf Hitler died by suicide on April 30, 1945, as Soviet forces closed in on Berlin. His death marked the end of the Nazi regime and World War II in Europe.

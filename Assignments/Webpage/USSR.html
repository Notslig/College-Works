1914 - Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand


In June 1914, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria was assassinated in Sarajevo, a Serbian nationalist an event that would trigger the outbreak of World War I.

Chain Reaction That Led to War:

  1. Austrian-Hungary blamed Serbia and declared war.
  2. Russia supported Serbia and mobilized its army.
  3. Germany supported Austria-Hungary and declared war on Russia.
  4. Germany declared war on France.
  5. Germany invaded Belgium to attack France.
  6. Britain entered the war to defend Belgium.


World War I – Eastern Front (1914 – 1917)

Imperial Russia Enters the Great War


Imperial Russia entered World War I as part of the Allied Powers. Russian forces fought Germany and Austria-Hungary on the Eastern Front, suffering heavy casualties due to poor logistics and leadership.

Impact: Military failures and economic strain destabilized the Russian Empire.



Russian Revolution (1917)

Collapse of the Russian Empire


The strain of World War I led to widespread unrest and revolution. In 1917, the Bolsheviks overthrew the provisional government, ending centuries of imperial rule.

Impact: Russia withdrew from World War I and moved toward a communist state.



Treaty of Brest-Litovsk (March 1918)

Russia Exits World War I


The Bolshevik government signed the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk with Germany, officially withdrawing Russia from World War I.

Russia lost significant territory but gained peace to consolidate power.

Impact: Allowed the formation of the Soviet Union.



Ended World War I

The signing of the Treaty of Versailles in 1919 officially ended World War I.



Formation of the USSR (1922)

Birth of the Soviet State


After years of civil war, the Bolsheviks emerged victorious. In 1922, the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR) was officially formed.

Impact: Established the world’s first communist superstate.



Operation Barbarossa (June 22, 1941)

The Great Patriotic War Begins


Nazi Germany launched a massive surprise invasion of the Soviet Union. Millions of Soviet soldiers and civilians were killed in the initial months.

Achievement: The USSR absorbed the attack and mobilized total war.



Battle of Moscow (1941–1942)

First Major German Defeat


German forces advanced toward Moscow but were halted by Soviet resistance, harsh winter conditions, and counteroffensives.

Achievement: Shattered the myth of German invincibility.



Battle of Stalingrad (1942–1943)

The Turning Point of World War II


The Battle of Stalingrad was one of the deadliest battles in history. Soviet forces encircled and destroyed the German Sixth Army.

Achievement: Marked the beginning of the Soviet offensive westward.



Battle of Kursk (July 1943)

Largest Tank Battle in History


The Battle of Kursk was the largest tank engagement ever fought. Soviet defenses absorbed the German assault and launched massive counterattacks.

Achievement: Eliminated Germany’s ability to launch large-scale offensives.



Liberation of Eastern Europe (1944)

Pushing the Nazis West


Soviet forces liberated large portions of Eastern Europe, driving German troops out of occupied territories.

Achievement: Expanded Soviet influence across Eastern Europe.



Battle of Berlin (April–May 1945)

The Fall of Nazi Germany


Soviet forces launched the final assault on Berlin. Intense street fighting led to the collapse of the Nazi regime.

Achievement: Captured Berlin and defeated Nazi Germany.



Victory in Europe (May 9, 1945)

The Great Patriotic War Ends


Germany surrendered unconditionally following the fall of Berlin. The Soviet Union emerged as one of the world’s two superpowers.

Achievement: Secured victory at immense human cost.



Victorious from World War II

The Soviet Union played a crucial role in the defeat of Nazi Germany, contributing to the Allied victory in Europe.

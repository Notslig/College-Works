1914 - Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand


In June 1914, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria was assassinated in Sarajevo, a Serbian nationalist an event that would trigger the outbreak of World War I.

Chain Reaction That Led to War:

  1. Austrian-Hungary blamed Serbia and declared war.
  2. Russia supported Serbia and mobilized its army.
  3. Germany supported Austria-Hungary and declared war on Russia.
  4. Germany declared war on France.
  5. Germany invaded Belgium to attack France.
  6. Britain entered the war to defend Belgium.


World War I (1914)

Japan Expands Influence


Japan joined the Allies and seized German colonies in Asia.

Achievement: Expanded Pacific control.



End of WWI

When the war ended Japan kept watching Germany and U.S. and made their moves



Attack on Pearl Harbor (1941)

War with the United States


Japan launched a surprise attack on the US Pacific Fleet.

Achievement: Temporary naval dominance.



Battle of Midway (1942)

Turning Point in the Pacific


Japan lost four aircraft carriers to the US Navy.

Impact: Loss of offensive capability.



Battle of Iwo Jima (1945)

Relentless Defense


Japanese forces fought to near total annihilation.

Casualties: ~20,000 Japanese dead.



Atomic Bombings (1945)

Unprecedented Destruction


Hiroshima and Nagasaki were devastated.

Outcome: Japan surrendered.



End of War

Utter defeat with unconditional surrender
